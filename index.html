How it works

  • Goal. Move the diamond-shaped robot onto the star tile.
  • Void kills. Stepping off the coloured path (into the dark background) ends the attempt for that try — hit Reset to put the robot back and try again.
  • Three functions. F1, F2 and F3 each hold a short sequence of instructions. F1 always runs first.
  • Colour-triggered calls. A "call F1/F2/F3" instruction only actually fires when the robot is currently standing on a tile of that same colour — otherwise it's skipped. This is how you branch.
  • Loop counters. Tap a placed call instruction to give it a use-limit (1–5) instead of unlimited (∞), so a self-call can repeat a fixed number of times — handy for long corridors.
  • The clock. One attempt = 90 minutes across every level. Your furthest level ever reached is saved on this device even after time runs out.
